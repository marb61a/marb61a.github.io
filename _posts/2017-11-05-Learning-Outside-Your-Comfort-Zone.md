**Some Links, Tips & Thoughts for the day**
**The Learning Comfort Zone**
  Most people regardless of the field that they are in tend to stick to what passes as broadly familiar when seeking to either
  broaden their skills or expand their knowledge. Nowhere is this more apparent than in IT where for example developers will
  spend hours and hours learning frameworks and libraries, languages and tools. This tends to give rise to a zone where the
  learner is fairly comfortable but shouldn't learning involve some discomfort, some sense of disturbance in the learner. This
  ties in with my ideas on Deliberate Practice where for example I think that in order to be a proper Web or Mobile Developer
  then I should know for example UX or Design more than I do. Its much easier to learn about any change in the newly released
  Angular 5 library than to fully appreciate the subtle and non-subtle complexities of colour theory and the affect on user
  experience, but I think that learning about more than what I am comforatble with will have a payoff.
  There are some great resources for learning about UI, UX and related material which are all free

        https://thegymnasium.com/courses
        https://webdesign.tutsplus.com/categories/ui-design
        https://www.smashingmagazine.com
        https://speckyboy.com
        https://hackdesign.org
  
  
  These are just some of the multitude of available resources and have some really great material as well as introducing
  some unfamiliar names and tools.
