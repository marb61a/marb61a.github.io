**Some Links, Tips & Thoughts for the day**

**PHP7**
  I have been doing some projects using PHP and something quite unexpected happened, I actually quite enjoyed it, it was not
  something that I expected but I like using the language itself. The frameworks that I am more familiar with are Wordpress
  and Laravel and I can have absolutely no complaints about performance levels. Some of the things that have been introduced
  in PHP 7 are every bit as good as the improvements JS has made over the last few years, for example you can now turn on
  type checking in a file very easily at the top of the file using
  
        declare(strict_types=1);

  
  This is very easy to do and having the ability to check types is very important for debugging any issues which will crop up,
  I was going to say may crop up but I don't know of a project anywhere in any technology where issues didn't crop up :)


**Wordpress**
  I know a lot of people are very disparaging toward the Wordpress platform in some quarters but I have never seen the logic
  as I have a lot of time for the platform as it can be very very useful and is also great for getting experience and 
  knowledge on sites and operations very quickly which can be useful far beyond the platform itself. I did have a look
  around and was surprised by the amount of sites the are Wordpress for people who are well known in the tech industry, if
  it is good enough for them then it is probably good enough for me. I only mention Wordpress as I have been learning about
  JavaScript and Wordpress and I think this will be a very propular combo for a while yet. I never knew that the Wordpress
  core was 30% JavaScript with the advent of Node being used a lot more on the platform it should attract even more 
  developers.
 
 
  
**Ubuntu** 
  So I am continuing to get amused and annoyed in equal measure by some of the things in Ubuntu 17.10, I did have to change 
  some things
  
  
  - The login screen has a seperate screen rotation lock which was a surprise, I smile now but.....

  - My browser has no minimize button

  - The screen lock was working even after being disabled and now doesn't after being re-enabled :)

  - I do get a warning on bootup for some reason, I tried sudo apt-get update as that worked previously but not now:)
 
 
 All in all I think that this is a step backwards for the distro as boot times are pretty poor so I would not recommend
 updating to anyone yet.
 
 
 **Bootcamps and Online Courses**
 Although I am a couple of months behind the times it did not surprise me in the least that some Web Development companies
 offering bootcamps are ceasing operations. I myself attended one on Android Development and it was very tough but very
 enjoyable. I did try to compare syllabus' from a few and there was no way that they would cover enough to go into even a
 junior positiion with confidence. There are some things that a lot of courses simply do not cover, what is worrying is that
 some instructors wear that lack of deep learning as a badge of honour and dismiss what would be needed learning as some 
 sort of unnecessary intellectual exercise, fortunately there are people like Eric Elliot, Kyle Simpson, Jafar Husain,
 Brad Traversy and Stephen Grider that not alone teach and share knowledge but actually do so to give the most benefit to
 anyone trying to learn, maybe if some schools had of tried this approach they would have been more successful.
