**Some Links, Tips & Thoughts for the day**
**Blackhat UX**
  I recently came across the concept of Blackhat UX, it was not something that I was familiar with all and was very curious
  and I had read a lot on Blackhat SEO and the various Blackhat security attacks. These are also referred to as Dark Patterns
  and the site I had a long read through is 
  
    https://darkpatterns.org
  
  It has not been updated in a while due to the controversy over some of the naming and shaming approach. Like anything else
  these patterns work by taking advantage of assumptions that the user makes and doing something that is not congruous with
  the user expectation. There are a few different patterns that nearly every web user would be familiar with
    
    Forced Continuity - This is where free trial periods end and charging begins without warning.
    Disguised Ads - Adverts disguised as other forms of content
    Bait & Switch - You set out to do one thing and another happens
  
  
  I think that the most interesting thing about this is how a lot of these patterns came about from trying to be more efficient
  and impove the user experience rather than any kind of nefarious motive, I wonder how many companies have actually read
  through the site and changed their UX strategy because of it, either way I would recommend anybody involved in development
  or design to read.
  
  PS
  As usual some of my most important learning comes from some independent sources rather than something I conciously set out
  to learn, in this case it was a UX position advertisement I was looking at. These little sources of knowledge and direction
  can be very important for padding out your understanding of how things work in the real world.


**Structured Data**
  I had read a lot about the concept of Structured Data, and was immensely curious about the concept. The first port of call
  was the excellent 2 part series on Moz.com 
    
    https://moz.com/blog/structured-data-for-seo-1
    https://moz.com/blog/structured-data-for-seo-2
  
  These articles as well as many more under the Technical SEO such as this one from Neil Patel
  
    https://neilpatel.com/blog/structured-data/
  
  are highly recommended. Essentially what Structured Data is, is the markup of the schema, what this does is improve the
  understanding of your site by Search Engines, this is because they can tell why content is in a particular place
  and is of a particular type. This can be very important to any site that is setting out to improve its SEO as the better
  a Search Engine understands your site the better it will perform in the SERP's.
  
  A lot of this stuff seems to tie in together, Accessibility, SEO, Structured Data and UX that is without going anywhere
  near the development stuff, this shows the complexity that can be involved in designing and developing a real world
  successful site. I wonder at this point how much actual invention or creativity is involved in the process of creating
  a site, considering that a lot of sites have to interact with various API, meet security standards, be responsive enough
  and performant enough to meet customer demands on load times and sell products or services which is what a lot have to
  do as a lot of sites are not for mere ornimental effect.
